Paper Abstract

Reinforcement Learning (RL) has become a widely applied method to applications of robotic manipulation. However, conventional approaches of this technique often disregard the non-Euclidean geometry inherent in pose representations, leading to inefficiencies in training and deployment. This paper introduces a framework that integrates Lie Group Theory into RL for robotic manipulation tasks, ensuring geometrically consistent treatment of poses. By mapping poses into Lie algebra and leveraging vectorized formulations, the method enables neural networks to process rotations and translations in a mathematically coherent manner. Multiple pose representations, including Euler angles, quaternions, transformation matrices, and dual quaternions, are evaluated within RL environments for reach, trajectory following, and pick-and-place tasks. Experiments demonstrate that the Dual Quaternion representation, when combined with Lie mappings, obtains superior accuracy and sim-to-real transfer compared to traditional formulations. The results highlight the benefits of embedding geometric structure in RL pipelines that use pose information as inputs and outputs, paving the way for more robust, efficient, and generalizable robotic manipulation strategies.
